# 04 Domain: Cultural Tourism Night Tour

## Primary Goals

Cultural tourism and night tour lighting should build a visitor journey, story sequence, emotional memory, and operation rhythm. It is not just brighter landscape lighting.

## Read Before Designing

Ask:
- What is the story source: local culture, myth, history, ecology, industry, art, festival, IP.
- What is the visitor path: entrance, prelude, discovery, climax, rest, exit.
- Which scenes are photo moments, which are quiet transitions.
- What media or interaction is justified by the story.
- How should crowd flow, safety, ticketing, and operation affect light scenes.

## Tool Box

- Scene chapter lighting: different zones have distinct emotional roles.
- Projection mapping: use on suitable surfaces when content and viewing distance justify it.
- Shadow storytelling: silhouettes, patterned screens, water mist, foliage, bamboo, lattice.
- Interactive light: pressure, sound, motion, touch, or timed response, only when maintenance is feasible.
- Low-brightness immersive path: guide visitors while preserving mystery.
- Stage-like focal moment: create one or two strong climaxes, not constant climax.
- Festival mode: higher saturation and motion for event periods, separate from daily mode.

## Constraints

- The story must be legible through sequence, not only through text labels.
- Maintain safe path visibility and crowd guidance.
- Do not let media content erase the site identity.
- Keep scenes maintainable for daily operation.
- Use late-night reduced mode for noise, spill, and energy control.

## Anti Patterns

- Every zone uses the same RGB gradient.
- Projection is added without story, surface, or viewing logic.
- The whole route stays at climax brightness.
- Cultural symbols become literal stickers rather than spatial experience.
- Interaction is proposed without maintenance or crowd-flow realism.

## Prompt Guidance

Use journey language: entrance prelude glow, quiet bamboo-shadow path, interactive ripple light near water, ceremonial warm focal scene, restrained dark transition, final viewpoint with layered skyline and cultural projection.
