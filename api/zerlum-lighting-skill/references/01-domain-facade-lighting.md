# 01 Domain: Facade Lighting

## Primary Goals

Facade lighting should reveal architecture, set a night identity, and fit the city context. It should not simply outline every edge or flood the whole building.

## Read Before Designing

Ask:
- What is the building type: commercial, hotel, office, cultural, residential, public, renovation.
- What is the dominant facade material: glass, stone, metal, concrete, brick, wood, mixed.
- What is the viewing distance: pedestrian close view, street view, skyline view, traffic corridor.
- What is the night role: landmark, quiet background, retail attraction, civic dignity, cultural expression.
- What should stay dark to create contrast.

## Tool Box

- Grazing light: emphasize stone, brick, concrete, relief, and vertical texture.
- Wall washing: create calm planes and reveal volume, but avoid flat over-lighting.
- Linear light: trace structure, rhythm, balcony, mullion, canopy, crown. Use selectively.
- Internal glow: use windows, atrium, lobby, translucent facade, or curtain wall depth.
- Point light: create sparkle, scale, and rhythm, especially where facade modules support it.
- Crown lighting: mark skyline presence without turning the roof into a disconnected object.
- Media facade: use only when architecture, brand, and operation justify dynamic content.

## Constraints

- Maintain original massing, perspective, facade proportion, and material identity.
- Control upward spill and visible glare.
- Avoid excessive saturation unless the project is commercial, festival, or media oriented.
- Use time scenes: daily, peak, late night, event.
- Coordinate brightness with nearby buildings, not only with the image itself.

## Anti Patterns

- Every edge is outlined with the same brightness.
- Blue-hour sky and cool facade are used regardless of user image.
- RGB color is applied without narrative or operation reason.
- Facade material is ignored and only decorative light strips are added.
- The building becomes brighter than its urban role requires.

## Prompt Guidance

When generating image prompts, use specific facade actions: reveal vertical stone texture with warm grazing light, create restrained internal lobby glow, keep upper residential floors quiet, emphasize entrance canopy with soft focal lighting.
