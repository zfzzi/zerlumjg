# 03 Domain: Landscape Lighting

## Primary Goals

Landscape lighting should provide safety, orientation, atmosphere, and ecological restraint. It should reveal paths, planting, water, terrain, and gathering nodes without turning the site into a theme-park glow.

## Read Before Designing

Ask:
- What landscape type is visible: park, plaza, garden, waterfront, resort, residential, entrance, courtyard.
- What is the visitor path and where people pause.
- Which elements deserve focus: tree canopy, trunks, water, rocks, sculpture, steps, signage, seating.
- What should remain quiet for ecological or visual balance.
- Is the atmosphere civic, intimate, resort-like, ceremonial, natural, or commercial.

## Tool Box

- Low bollard or shielded path light: guide walking while limiting glare.
- Tree uplight: use sparingly; choose trunk, canopy, or silhouette, not everything.
- Moonlighting: hide light high in trees to create natural shadow on ground.
- Step and handrail light: safety with low visual noise.
- Under-bench and under-edge light: floating effect and soft guidance.
- Water reflection light: use angle and surface reflection, avoid harsh source visibility.
- Rock and planting grazing: reveal texture and depth.
- Lantern or localized decorative light: use when cultural atmosphere supports it.

## Constraints

- Avoid glare at pedestrian eye level.
- Keep path and step safety clear.
- Respect dark zones, planting health, wildlife, and nearby residences.
- Hide fixtures where possible.
- Do not make all trees green or all water blue by default.

## Anti Patterns

- Every tree receives the same uplight.
- Green light is applied to planting as a default.
- Path lights are too bright and dominate the scene.
- Water features become over-saturated blue without reason.
- Landscape lighting ignores circulation and only decorates objects.

## Prompt Guidance

Use landscape-specific language: shielded low path lights, soft amber light under benches, selective trunk grazing, moonlight-like canopy shadows, subtle water reflection, dark planting background for depth.
