# 05 Typology Playbook: Hotel Lobby

## Use

Use this file when the project is an interior hotel lobby, boutique hotel entrance hall, resort reception, or hospitality arrival space.

## Guiding Questions

- Besides the reception desk, what is the real visual center: art installation, staircase, water feature, lounge, bar, courtyard view, ceiling, or material wall.
- Should the first impression be grand, quiet, local, luxurious, intimate, resort-like, or artful.
- How should the brightness transition from entrance to reception to elevator or lounge guide emotion.
- Which material deserves close light interaction: stone, wood, bamboo, metal, fabric, glass, plants, water.
- Should the decorative lighting object be visible, or should light feel hidden and atmospheric.

## Strategies

- Arrival sequence: make the doorway readable, then guide the eye to reception and the main spatial feature.
- Reception task: provide functional visibility while making the counter material and brand wall feel refined.
- Lounge comfort: use lower contrast, warm pools of light, and shielded sources.
- Ceiling strategy: avoid filling the ceiling with random downlights. Use coves, slots, pendants, or shadow depending on style.
- Local culture: translate culture into material, pattern, shadow, rhythm, and journey rather than literal icon stacking.

## Avoid

- Defaulting to a large crystal chandelier.
- Overusing downlights until the lobby feels like a showroom.
- Making every wall equally bright.
- Ignoring guest comfort at seating zones.
- Applying exterior night-render language to an indoor hospitality scene.

## Example Direction Pattern

For a Chengdu bamboo boutique hotel lobby, a strong direction might be: use bamboo shadow as the main memory, concealed warm cove light as the ambient layer, grazing light on bamboo and stone as focal texture, soft table-level light in lounge areas, and a restrained reception glow as the service anchor.
