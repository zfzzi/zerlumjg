# 02 Domain: Interior Lighting

## Primary Goals

Interior lighting should support function, comfort, material quality, and spatial emotion. It should not default to exterior night-scene language.

## Read Before Designing

Ask:
- What space is this: lobby, restaurant, hotel room, retail, office, exhibition, residence, corridor, atrium.
- What activities happen here and where people look first.
- What materials dominate: stone, wood, metal, fabric, glass, art, plants, water.
- What mood is intended: quiet, ceremonial, social, efficient, intimate, retail-active.
- Which surfaces can carry light without glare.

## Tool Box

- Indirect cove light: soft ambient base and ceiling/wall depth.
- Downlight: functional task visibility, use beam control and spacing discipline.
- Wall washer: make walls visible, expand perceived space, support art and texture.
- Accent spotlight: highlight front desk, table, artwork, sculpture, plant, merchandise.
- Grazing light: reveal rough stone, bamboo, brick, timber, textile, relief.
- Linear concealed light: define steps, counters, shelves, ceiling slots, handrails.
- Pendant or chandelier: use as spatial object only when style supports it.
- Backlight and translucent material: make glass, stone, acrylic, fabric, and screens glow.

## Constraints

- Avoid direct glare from visible LEDs, downlights, and reflected surfaces.
- Keep task areas readable: counter, dining table, circulation, display, signage.
- Maintain color rendering for skin, food, art, textile, and wood.
- Coordinate color temperature with material: low warm for intimate wood/stone, neutral warm for modern hospitality, cooler only when concept demands it.
- Do not overfill the whole room. Interior quality often comes from controlled contrast.

## Anti Patterns

- Uniform ceiling downlights with no wall brightness or focal hierarchy.
- Decorative chandeliers inserted into every lobby regardless of style.
- Blue-hour sky, exterior landscape, or facade wording applied to indoor images.
- Every strip light is visible and equally bright.
- Warm color temperature is used as a slogan without explaining material or mood.

## Prompt Guidance

For indoor images, describe interior light sources and spatial layers: concealed warm cove light washing the ceiling edge, focused spotlight on reception stone texture, soft wall washer on artwork, low-glare downlights guiding circulation, warm reflection on wood and metal.
